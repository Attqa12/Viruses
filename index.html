What Are Viruses?

A virus is a microscopic infectious agent that can only replicate within the living cells of a host organism. They are not considered "alive" in the traditional sense, as they cannot carry out the biological processes required for reproduction, energy production, or metabolism on their own. Instead, viruses hijack the machinery of a host cell to reproduce and create more virus particles (virions).
