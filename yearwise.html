2020 - COVID-19

The COVID-19 pandemic originated in Wuhan, China, caused by the SARS-CoV-2 virus. It quickly became a global pandemic, affecting millions worldwide. Key symptoms include fever, cough, and difficulty breathing. Vaccines and strict preventive measures have helped control the spread.


Learn More

2013 - Chikungunya

Chikungunya refers to the significant global outbreak of Chikungunya virus (CHIKV), which spread rapidly across multiple regions, including the Caribbean, the Americas, Southeast Asia, and the Indian Ocean. Chikungunya is a viral disease transmitted primarily by Aedes mosquitoes, particularly Aedes aegypti and Aedes albopictus.


Learn More

2010 - Dengue

Dengue, a viral infection transmitted by Aedes mosquitoes, saw a significant surge in cases globally in 2010, with widespread outbreaks in many tropical and subtropical regions. The 2009 dengue outbreaks caused a dramatic increase in the number of reported cases, with millions affected worldwide.


Learn More

2009 - H1N1 Swine Flu

H1N1 influenza, commonly known as Swine Flu, originated in Mexico and spread rapidly, becoming a global pandemic. It primarily affected younger populations, causing respiratory symptoms and, in severe cases, pneumonia.


Learn More

1918 - Spanish Flu

The Spanish Flu pandemic was one of the deadliest in history, infecting over 500 million people worldwide. Caused by the H1N1 influenza virus, it had a significant impact on global populations during World War I.
